§ 1.2
Project Overview
The Problem We Solve
The data Zillow has no incentive to show you

PITI — Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance — is the true monthly cost of homeownership. Lenders calculate it. Escrow accounts collect it. The single most volatile component, insurance, is routinely estimated rather than verified at the point of purchase decision. In insurance-stressed markets, Zillow's standard estimate can be more than $1,000 per month wrong.

When State Farm stops writing policies in a county, that signal is the most accurate leading indicator of future property value decline available anywhere — and it arrives months before any map, model, or Zestimate reflects it. That signal is never surfaced by platforms whose revenue depends on transaction volume. Possessio's incentive is structurally opposite: the protocol earns from use, not from sales.

Zillow shows you comps. Possessio shows you what actually determines long-term value — real-time insurance intelligence layered on every property in America, listed or not.
The Gap
In coastal Florida, fire-corridor California, and flood-zone Texas, the difference between Zillow's estimated PITI and the True PITI can exceed $1,380/month. That delta affects debt-to-income ratios, mortgage qualification, and real affordability. The lender finds this number at underwriting. Possessio shows it before the offer.
The Signal
Insurance carrier withdrawals are the earliest structural signal in the property market. When a carrier exits a county, every listing in that county is mispriced against the new insurance reality. Possessio aggregates this signal across all 50 states in real time. Nobody else does this at this layer.
The Fix
An open, protocol-funded public intelligence index. No login required. No token required to view data. Institutional-grade feeds, funded by the L.A.T.E. treasury. The index replaces the traditional gated listing system with a free public data layer — permanently.

§ 2.2
The L.A.T.E. Contract
L — Liquidity Pillar
25% of ETH swap fees · Paired with $PITI · Injected into Base Liquidity Pool

The Liquidity Pillar has two inputs — not one. First: 25% of all ETH swap fees are automatically paired with $PITI and injected directly into the Base Liquidity Pool. Second: 25% of the yield earned by the treasury on its yield-bearing assets is also injected into the LP. Both streams continuously deepen the pool. This is not rented liquidity — it is owned by the protocol permanently, growing from two compounding sources simultaneously.

The compounding effect is structural: each fee cycle deepens the pool, generates more LP fee yield, and raises the price floor. The injection function is hardcoded. No admin key, governance vote, or multi-sig action can redirect these funds. The 25% path is immutable from deployment.

Liquidity Flow — Two Input Streams
Input 1: 25% of ETH swap fees — captured by L.A.T.E. on every swap event
Input 2: 25% of treasury yield — earned on cbETH / Ondo USDY deployed by the treasury
Pairing: ETH paired with $PITI at current pool ratio
Destination: Base Liquidity Pool — protocol-owned LP position
Secondary yield: LP fees generated by pool activity, reinvested into LP
Effect: Continuously rising price floor, deepening pool, lower slippage for users
Immutability requirement: The liquidity injection function must be hardcoded at deployment. No admin function, upgrade path, or governance mechanism may alter the 25% routing logic post-deploy.
§ 2.3
The L.A.T.E. Contract
T — Treasury Pillar
75% of ETH swap fees · Safe (Gnosis) 3-of-5 Multi-Sig · API operations budget

The Treasury Pillar routes 75% of all ETH swap fees to a Safe (Gnosis) multi-sig wallet governed by a 3-of-5 signing structure. These funds are then deployed into yield-bearing assets — cbETH or Ondo USDY. The yield earned is split: 25% back into the LP as an additional liquidity injection, 75% retained in the treasury to fund API subscriptions and operations. The treasury is the protocol's operating budget and its yield engine simultaneously.

The treasury is the reason the index can be zero-cost to users. The data is expensive at institutional tier. The L.A.T.E. treasury pays for it autonomously, without advertising, without selling user data, and without any human discretion over fund deployment beyond what the 3-of-5 governance structure permits.

Treasury Flow
Input: 75% of ETH swap fees — deployed into cbETH or Ondo USDY
Yield split: 25% of yield → LP injection · 75% of yield → treasury operations
Destination: Safe (Gnosis) multi-sig — 3-of-5 signing threshold
Primary use: Institutional API subscriptions — insurance withdrawal, county rate, FAIR Plan, FEMA feeds
Effect: Treasury compounds continuously — yield funds both the LP and API costs simultaneously
Governance: Any treasury disbursement requires 3-of-5 signers — subject to 48-hour timelock (see §4.4)
§ 2.4
The L.A.T.E. Contract
A — Asset Yield Pillar
75% of swap fees deployed to yield · 25% of yield back into LP · 75% of yield funds operations

The 75% treasury allocation is deployed into yield-bearing assets — cbETH or Ondo USDY. The yield earned on these assets is then split: 25% of yield is injected back into the LP, adding to the liquidity floor on top of the direct swap fee injection. 75% of yield is retained in the treasury to fund API subscriptions and compound further. The result is a treasury that never sits idle — it funds operations and simultaneously deepens the liquidity pool from two directions.

This means the treasury is never idle. Every dollar of surplus is working. Yield compounds back into the treasury, extending the protocol's API runway without requiring additional fee volume. As usage grows, yield grows — and the protocol becomes progressively more self-sustaining.

cbETH
Coinbase Wrapped Staked ETH. ETH-denominated yield from Ethereum proof-of-stake consensus rewards. 25% of yield earned flows back into the LP. 75% funds treasury operations.
Ondo USDY
Tokenized yield-bearing stablecoin backed by U.S. Treasury Bills. Stable-value yield without ETH price exposure. Same 25/75 yield split applies.

The DEED Score
A 0–100 composite built on the four signals that determine long-term property value

The DEED Score is applied to every indexed property — listed or off-market. It is a signal-weighted composite of four sub-scores, each out of 25, calibrated to the factors that institutional insurers and mortgage lenders already use to price risk. The score is not a marketing rating. It is a structured summary of the insurance viability, climate resilience, geographic stability, and market accuracy of any given property.

🌊 Climate
/ 25 pts
Flood risk, wildfire probability, water access security, and heat stress exposure. Sourced from FEMA flood zone data, carrier risk models, and climate migration datasets. The most predictive long-run sub-score — insurance markets price climate first, then the property market follows.
🛡️ Insurance
/ 25 pts
Number of active carriers in the county, withdrawal history over 48 months, FAIR Plan dependency rate, and year-over-year premium trend. Sourced from live institutional carrier filing data funded by the L.A.T.E. treasury. The earliest structural signal of value decline.
📍 Geography
/ 25 pts
Economic anchor quality, employer diversity, infrastructure resilience, and climate migration inflow/outflow patterns. A university-anchored Great Lakes city with net inbound migration scores near 25. A single-employer coastal town with net outmigration and rising FAIR Plan dependency scores low.
📊 Market
/ 25 pts
How accurately the current list price reflects insurance-adjusted fundamentals. When the DEED Estimate diverges significantly from the Zestimate, this sub-score explains why — and quantifies how much of the gap is insurance risk not yet priced by backward-looking comps.

§ 4.1
Security & Fail-Safe
Immutable Liquidity Logic
The 25% liquidity injection function is hardcoded — no admin can redirect these funds

This is the protocol's primary security requirement. The 25% ETH fee allocation to the Base Liquidity Pool must be hardcoded in the L.A.T.E. contract at deployment. There must be no admin function, no onlyOwner modifier, no upgrade proxy, and no governance mechanism that can alter or bypass this routing logic after deployment.

The immutability of the liquidity path is what makes the protocol trustless at the liquidity layer. Users, token holders, and institutional API partners can rely on the price floor continuously rising — because the mechanism that drives it cannot be turned off.

Developer Implementation Requirement
The liquidity injection function must not be wrapped in any conditional that can be toggled by an admin address. It must not be located behind a proxy contract. It must not be part of any function set that can be upgraded, paused (except by the Emergency Circuit Breaker — §4.3), or replaced. The 25% routing must be verifiable by reading the deployed bytecode directly, with no intermediary trust required.
§ 4.2
Security & Fail-Safe
Multi-Sig Governance
75% treasury governed by a Safe (Gnosis) 3-of-5 multi-sig

The 75% treasury allocation is received by a Safe (Gnosis) multi-sig wallet requiring 3-of-5 signers to authorize any outbound transaction. This structure ensures that no single key compromise, no single team member, and no external actor can unilaterally access treasury funds.

Threshold
3-of-5 signatures required for any treasury disbursement. Standard for institutional-grade DeFi treasury management on Base.
Scope
Governs all outbound treasury transactions: API subscription payments and any protocol parameter changes subject to the 48-hour timelock.
Transparency
All multi-sig transactions are publicly visible on the Safe UI and on-chain. The treasury address is published in the System Logic Reference (§6.3). Any holder can monitor balance, pending transactions, and signer activity at all times.
§ 4.3
Security & Fail-Safe
Emergency Circuit Breaker
Halts fee-routing if the connected DEX or network experiences a critical exploit

The L.A.T.E. contract must include an emergency pause function capable of halting all fee-routing activity — without affecting the treasury balance, the yield position, or the Liquidity Pool LP position already deployed. The circuit breaker is a defensive mechanism only: it stops new fees from being routed during an active exploit. It does not touch existing deployed funds.

Trigger Conditions
Critical exploit on the connected Base DEX; abnormal fee volume indicative of sandwich or reentrancy attack; network-level emergency declared by Base foundation; or 3-of-5 multi-sig manual trigger within the 48-hour timelock exception window.
What Pauses
New fee capture and routing only. The 25% LP injection and 75% treasury routing are suspended. Existing LP positions, treasury balance, and yield deployments are untouched.
Resume Condition
Resumption requires 3-of-5 multi-sig authorization. The exploit must be confirmed resolved. Resumption itself is subject to the 48-hour timelock (§4.4).
§ 4.4
Security & Fail-Safe
48-Hour Timelock
Any changes to protocol parameters require a 48-hour delay before execution

All changes to protocol parameters — fee percentages, yield target contracts, governance signer set, circuit breaker state — must pass through a 48-hour timelock before execution. This means any proposed change is publicly visible on-chain for 48 hours before it can take effect. Any user, holder, or observer can see a proposed change and act accordingly before it becomes live.

Timelock Scope
The 48-hour timelock applies to: (1) any change to the fee-split percentages; (2) any change to the Safe multi-sig signer set or threshold; (4) resumption of fee-routing after an emergency pause; (5) any upgrade to non-immutable contract components. The timelock does not apply to routine treasury disbursements for API costs, which are authorized by the 3-of-5 multi-sig under normal operating conditions.
