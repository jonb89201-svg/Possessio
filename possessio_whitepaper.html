§ 1.1
What Was Built
What POSSESSIO Is
An open-source, MIT-licensed deterministic treasury engine any business or organization can use freely.

PLATE.sol is an open-source smart contract deployed on Base that autonomously manages protocol funds through a certified fee-routing engine. It collects a 2% fee on every $PITI token swap and routes it automatically — 25% into permanent protocol-owned liquidity, 75% into a multi-sig treasury that deploys to ETH staking. No human discretion is required or possible in the core fee-routing path.

The contract and framework are fully public and reusable. The L.A.T.E. framework is MIT licensed — no permission required. Deployment and full integration services are available separately. POSSESSIO's first planned application layer is a free public property intelligence index funded by treasury yield — that layer is roadmap, not yet built.

What Was Built
Origin & Build Context
Built entirely on a smartphone in two weeks for approximately $300

POSSESSIO was built by a single developer working between shifts at a grocery store in Omaha, Nebraska, while caring for a family member. The entire protocol was built on a $50 Android smartphone using mobile-native tooling. No desktop. No development environment. No team.

The protocol was built to solve a real problem: most businesses with token-denominated transaction volume have no reliable, non-discretionary way to convert fees into permanent treasury value. The smart contract mechanics were designed to do this autonomously — eliminating the need for manual treasury management, human routing decisions, or trusted intermediaries.

Design Principles
Non-discretionary. Self-executing. Transparent by architecture.
Non-Discretionary
Fee routing and yield deployment are handled by self-executing smart contract code, not human management. The 25% liquidity split is hardcoded and immutable. No admin key, governance vote, or multi-sig action can redirect these funds.
Transparent

§ 2.1
PLATE.sol
L.A.T.E. Architecture
Liquidity and Treasury Engine — the autonomous smart contract on Base

PLATE.sol is the Protocol Liquidity Asset Treasury Engine. It is an ERC20 token contract that intercepts a 2% fee on every token transfer and routes it through a four-pillar system. The routing logic is hardcoded and immutable. No human decision-making is required or possible in the core fee path.

§ 2.2
PLATE.sol
Fee Mechanics
2% flat fee · ETH-denominated · Collected on every transfer

Every transfer of $PITI tokens triggers the fee collection function. The fee is 2% of the transfer amount, collected in $PITI and accumulated in a pending fee pool. When the pool reaches the minimum swap batch threshold, fees are converted to ETH via Aerodrome and routed through the 25/75 split.

Fee Mechanics — From the Contract
FEE_BPS = 200 (2% flat fee on every transfer)
FEE_DENOM = 10,000
LP_PCT = 25 (25% of ETH fees → Aerodrome LP, immutable)
TREASURY_PCT = 75 (75% of ETH fees → Treasury Safe)
DAI_BOOT_PCT = 20 (20% of treasury → DAI until target met)
DAI_TARGET = $2,280 (stable reserve floor — 20 of 20% bootstrap)
SWAP_DELAY = 24 hours (MEV protection between fee swaps)
Standard: ERC20 + ERC20Permit (gasless approvals supported)
Why ETH-Denominated
Dollar strengthens: ETH buys more operational capacity in USD terms
Dollar weakens: ETH appreciates — same treasury balance funds more in dollar terms
Staking yield: Driven by network activity, not Federal Reserve policy decisions
Result: No bad macro scenario for an ETH-denominated treasury funding USD-cost operations
§ 2.3
PLATE.sol
Treasury Architecture
75% of ETH fees · DAI reserve · Diversified staking · 3-of-5 Safe

The treasury is governed by a Safe (Gnosis) 3-of-5 multi-sig wallet. It is denominated in ETH. The 75% of swap fees that route to the treasury deploy through a three-layer system designed for both stability and growth.

Treasury — Three Layers
Layer 1 — DAI Emergency Reserve: 20% of the 75% treasury allocation flows to DAI until the reserve reaches $2,280. This is the minimum viable stable buffer. Auto-switches to full staking when target is met. Auto-refills if drawn down. Hardcoded quantity check — not a price oracle. No public withdrawal function.
Layer 2 — Diversified ETH Staking: Remaining treasury deploys across three providers: 20% cbETH (Coinbase), 40% wstETH (Lido), 40% rETH (Rocket Pool). No single provider holds more than 50%. Emergency exit protocol: Chainlink cbETH/ETH feed deviation greater than 3% triggers automatic pause of new cbETH deposits.
Layer 3 — Continuous Compounding: $PITI swap fees continuously add ETH to the treasury. ETH is never sold — always staked. Treasury grows in ETH terms perpetually.
Staking Allocation — From the Contract
CBETH_PCT = 20 (20% of staking allocation → cbETH)
WSTETH_PCT = 40 (40% → wstETH)
RETH_PCT = 40 (40% → rETH)
DEPEG_THRESH = 97% (3% depeg triggers cbETH pause)
YIELD_TO_LP = 25 (25% of yield → LP)
YIELD_TO_T = 75 (75% of yield → treasury)
Blended APY: ~3.0% (cbETH 20% / wstETH 40% / rETH 40%)

§ 3.1
Security
MEV & Sandwich Protection
24-hour swap delay · TickMath TWAP oracle · Both enforced at the contract level

PLATE.sol defends against the two most common attack vectors in DeFi fee management: front-running (MEV) and sandwich attacks.

MEV Protection
Mechanism: 24-hour mandatory delay between fee swaps (SWAP_DELAY = 24 hours)
Effect: Bots that front-run pending fee swaps cannot exploit predictable swap timing
Enforcement: Hardcoded in the contract — cannot be reduced without governance timelock
Sandwich Attack Protection
Mechanism: TickMath TWAP price oracle (Uniswap V3 library, MIT License)
Effect: Calculates minimum acceptable output before any swap executes — artificial price manipulation is rejected
Bootstrap: Manual reference price used during first 24 hours after pool creation while TWAP observation history builds
Symmetry Guard: 5% TWAP deviation halts execution (maxDeviationBps = 500)
§ 3.2
Security
48-Hour Timelock
All parameter changes queued publicly · No instant modifications · Not even by the owner

No parameter in PLATE.sol can be changed instantly. Every governance action — updating the LP address, adding a DEX pair, resuming after a pause, executing an emergency exit — must be queued 48 hours in advance. The queue is public and on-chain. Anyone can see what's coming before it executes.

Timelock — From the Contract
TIMELOCK = 48 hours
Applies to: LP address updates · DEX pair additions · Resume routing · cbETH emergency exit
Queue function: returns bytes32 ID that must match on execution
Mismatched parameters → revert (cannot queue one action, execute another)
§ 3.3
Security
Depeg Monitor & Circuit Breaker
Chainlink feed monitoring · Automatic pause · Emergency halt
cbETH Depeg Monitor
Feed: Chainlink cbETH/ETH price oracle
Threshold: DEPEG_THRESH = 97% (3% below peg triggers pause)
Action: Automatic pause of new cbETH deposits — no human required
Resume: Automatic when price recovers above threshold
Full exit: Requires 3-of-5 governance vote + 48-hour timelock
Circuit Breaker
Function: pauseRouting() — owner callable · instant effect
Effect: Halts fee routing only — does not touch deployed staking positions
Resume: queueResumeRouting() → 48-hour timelock → resumeRouting()
Design intent: Fast to pause. Slow to restart. Emergency brake does not trap funds.
